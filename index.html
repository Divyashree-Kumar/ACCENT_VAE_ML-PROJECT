VAE Accent Classification

Speaker Accent Recognition with Variational Autoencoders

Project Overview

Objective

Demonstrate how a Variational Autoencoder (VAE) can generate synthetic training data that improves the performance of speaker accent classification models on a small MFCC feature dataset.

Dataset

Total samples: 329
Features: 12 MFCC coefficients
Classes: 6 accents (ES, FR, GE, IT, UK, US)

VAE Summary (Phase 2)

Architecture and Training

Encoder: 12 → 8 → 2 (latent dimensions)
Decoder: 2 → 8 → 12
Training epochs: 100
Final average loss: approximately 7.16
Loss: Mean squared reconstruction loss plus KL divergence regularization.

Interpretation

Training with VAE-generated synthetic samples consistently improved all three classifiers relative to the original-only training set, and performed better than simple random duplication. This indicates that the VAE learned a useful representation of the accent distribution and generated informative synthetic MFCC feature vectors.

Conclusion

Summary

The project shows that a VAE can be used as an effective data augmentation tool for speaker accent recognition. By generating synthetic MFCC feature vectors, the VAE slightly but consistently improves classification accuracy over a strong baseline on a small dataset.
